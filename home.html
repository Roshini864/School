Welcome to Meridian School

Welcome To Meridian School

A sacred space where knowledge and culture coexist, old world values with futuristic approach dwell in the annals. Established in the year 1995, Meridian School stands as a magnificent building in the midst of lofty Banjara Hills. Meridian instills love for learning, infuses values for living.

Best School CBSE

Committed to empower children to think independently and adapt to new environs, to create balanced thinkers who can contribute to the progress of humanity.

Best IB PYP School

Meridian works towards the holistic development of the students by providing them with multiple opportunities and pathways to self-discovery.

Meridian is a New Generation Educational Group committed to educate children for life and thus bring a change in the society. Learning philosophy practised at Meridian infuses traditional values into a progressive approach, equipping students to adapt and excel anywhere in the world. Our alumni, working in diverse fields, have found Meridian to be the best school for a successful career take off.


Meridian School, Madhapur Campus welcomed students aboard for the first time in 2006. Following the CBSE curriculum and implementing a wide range of extra-curricular activities, school now has strength of 1400+ children from grades Nursery to Class XII, making it the Best School in Hi-Tech city.

Swati & Rameshwar Jamwal

parent

What defines a good school? As defined by Joanne Yatvin , a good school is a school that creates a sense of community that permits personal expression within a framework of social responsibility. It focuses on learnings that grow through use, such as communication skills, decision making, craftsmanship and group interaction. It makes children think for themselves as people who find strength, nourishment and joy in learning. Our journey at Meridian started when Krittika joined the school in 2007 as a ‘mid-term’ admission in PP1 ( pri primary). I remember, the day, as clearly as though it happened yesterday. When the interview was done and Usha Reddy Ma’am, asked me  when Krittika could join the school, I had queried, whether Krittika would be required to go through an assessment. She looked at Krittika and asked her “Do you want to join my school?” Krittika, her eyes shining and wearing her widest smile ever, just nodded. Ma’am then said  “that’s all we need - big bright eyes and a wide smile. The rest is for us to do.” That sentence defines our journey. Krittika is poised to leave school next year. She’s still got her big bright smile and shining eyes intact. Through the years whether it was camping in school, going on field trips, participating in various events, singing & dancing on stage, giving exams, being coached when the results weren’t great to sharing high fives when they were, representing the school in sporting & cultural events, to participating in the MUN and holding leadership roles like the sports secretary and most recently the head girl, she’s experienced it all.  She has grown and blossomed into this confident child who has found strength, nourishment and joy in learning at Meridian. As we look back, we feel so good that our child is a Meridianite, where she has been taught to connect her heart and her mind to all that she does.  Along with her I have seen her classmates become incredible young people. I’ve also marveled at how the teaching staff, admin staff and heads of school  don’t seem to age. Thank you all for being incredible. For helping us cope as a family as we went through our biggest personal loss. To ensure a child is taken care off even outside the school environment, to support and protect the mental well-being of a child and his/ her family. You have demonstrated it all in our time at the school. Class teachers, subject teachers, non-teaching staff, admin staff – how does one even begin to say thank you?  I believe as Krittika leaves school and starts her next leg, through her actions and contribution she will attempt  to say “ Thank you – Meridian school for Boys and Girls, Banjara Hills - for everything!” Swati & Rameshwar Jamwal Parents of Krittika Jamwal – (PP1 – XII)What defines a good school?
